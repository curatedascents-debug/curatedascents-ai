## What Makes a Great Shot for the Website

The website is designed for high-end travellers — every image should feel cinematic and aspirational. A few things that make a photo work really well:

- **Wide, dramatic landscapes** — mountain ridges, valleys, glaciers, rivers with peaks in the background. These are the most used across destination pages and the homepage.
- **Golden hour and blue hour** — sunrise and sunset shots have a warmth that works beautifully on the site. If you have these, please include them and label the folder accordingly.
- **Clear subjects** — avoid shots where the main subject is too small or lost in the frame. The website crops and resizes images, so a clean composition holds up better.
- **Horizontal (landscape) orientation** — the site is designed around wide images, so horizontal shots are preferred. Portrait shots are welcome too, especially for people and hotel room photos.
- **Authentic moments** — locals going about daily life, guides leading treks, tea houses on the trail. These work well alongside the landscape shots to give the destination pages a human feel.

No need to edit or colour-grade anything — just share the originals and we will handle that on our end.

## What to Avoid

- Heavily cropped or low-resolution photos (anything below 1920 × 1080 pixels may not be usable at full size)
- Blurry or out-of-focus shots
- Photos with visible watermarks or date stamps
- Screenshots from social media (quality is too low)

## Hero Video — Homepage Background

The most important video we need is a **short cinematic clip for the homepage background**. This plays silently and loops behind the main heading when someone first lands on the site.

What works best:

- A slow aerial or panning shot over the Himalaya — mountain ridges, glaciers, valleys with clouds rolling through
- Sunrise or golden hour over Everest or Annapurna (time-lapse or real-time slow pan)
- A wide shot of a trekking route with people silhouetted against a dramatic sky
- Slow-motion prayer flags in the wind with peaks in the background

The clip should feel calm and majestic — smooth, continuous motion works best since it loops. Fast cuts or action shots don't work as a background.

**Technical requirements:**
- Format: MP4
- Resolution: 1920 × 1080 minimum (4K preferred if you have it)
- Duration: 15–90 seconds
- Horizontal/landscape only

Please put this in the `videos/` folder and label it clearly, e.g. `hero-video-annapurna-sunrise.mp4`.
